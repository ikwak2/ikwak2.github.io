About me

I am a Data Scientist working at Samsung Research; research in AI security.

I received BS in mathematics in 2006, from Korea University, and a MS in statistics in 2008, under supervison of Myung-Hoe Huh. I got a PhD in statistics in 2014, from the University of Wisconsin-Madison; My PhD advisor was Karl Broman. I was a postdoctoral associate with Wei Pan and Daniel Garry at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities, 2014-2017. I am now working at Samsung Research from 2017.

I am applied statistician focusing on problems in AI security and statistical genetics. I am eager to design new methods for big and complicated data. I am also excited to develop new useable tools for the method so that those methods can be widely adopted and used by other researchers.

  • Real-world big data analysis: Develop new service that combines enterprise big data
  • Biostatistics: statistical methodology and software development for bio-data analysis
  • Development of Statistical Software tools
  • Deep learning applications
